# MindWP page planning

This guide helps a capable planner define or review page meaning. It is not a workflow, a page catalogue or a visual brief. Page-specific plans remain separate and are supplied only when the relevant page is being planned or executed.

Foundation and Strategy own the wider business. Writing owns expression. Design owns visual interpretation. A page plan applies those authorities to one visitor decision without redefining them.

## When research is useful

Research should answer a named uncertainty: audience behaviour, search or task intent, terminology, factual context, available proof, professional boundaries, competitors or current conditions. Separate sourced evidence from inference and from a decision the user still needs to make.

Do not research for ceremony. Existing authority and supplied evidence may already resolve the page. For unstable, specialist or high-stakes claims, use current primary sources when research is in scope.

## Define the page's responsibility

A useful plan establishes:

- the page identity and single primary job;
- the audience and their search or task intent;
- the decision or action the visitor should be ready to take;
- which offer or customer moment the page owns;
- what belongs on another page;
- the recognised situation, honest promise and relevant objections;
- the narrative progression from arrival to action;
- the real proof available;
- claims, consent, privacy and professional boundaries;
- what is fixed, adaptable, open or unavailable.

Search intent is not merely a keyword. It includes what the visitor is trying to understand, compare, verify or do and what evidence would satisfy that task.

## Plan meaning, not components

A plan row is meaning material. It does not prescribe a card, panel, split layout, tab, timeline, diagram, interface, animation or other component.

A planned section is not automatically one horizontal visual band. Several related meanings may share an environment when they form one continuous experience. One major meaning may need substantial development; a quiet transition may need only clear language and space. Design determines those relationships.

Headings may be planned because they sharpen the argument. They remain adaptable unless explicitly marked fixed. Preserve approved facts, claims, search intent, genuine proof, boundaries and fixed wording; leave explanatory depth, grouping and supporting copy adaptable where possible.

Supporting detail is a source of meaning, not a required public-copy or visual-object count. The executor may shorten, deepen, regroup or rewrite adaptable material as content and composition develop. The executor must not invent missing strategy, proof, claims or operational detail.

## Compact page-brief contract

Use as much of this structure as the page needs. Straightforward pages may use prose; complex pages may use a compact table.

### Page boundary

- **Identity and job:** page name, purpose and the decision it helps.
- **Audience and search/task intent:** who arrives, why and what they need to evaluate.
- **Desired action:** the primary next step and any justified lower-commitment path.
- **Offer and cross-page ownership:** what this page owns, what it may mention and what belongs elsewhere.
- **Narrative progression:** the sequence of understanding, trust, evidence and action.

### Each section or genuinely connected group

- **Heading:** a working or fixed heading.
- **Broad job:** what this part contributes to the page argument.
- **Essential meaning:** what must remain understandable.
- **Available proof:** real media, work, evidence, process or source material.
- **Relevant boundary:** claims, privacy, consent, professional or service-ownership limit.
- **Status:** which material is fixed, adaptable, open or unavailable.

### Supplied and unresolved material

- **Global supplied evidence:** research, assets, source material and permissions that apply across the page.
- **Global fixed and adaptable material:** facts or wording that affect several sections.
- **Material unresolved decisions:** only decisions that would materially change this page; identify their owner and do not invent an answer.

Status meanings:

- **Verbatim fixed:** preserve the exact approved wording.
- **Meaning or fact fixed:** preserve the approved substance while allowing responsible wording changes.
- **Adaptable:** may be rewritten, regrouped, shortened, deepened or reformatted while preserving its job and truth.
- **Open:** requires a decision or evidence before that claim or meaning can be final; mark it **blocking** only when the page cannot proceed responsibly, otherwise **deferrable**.
- **Unavailable:** must not be simulated, especially as proof.

There is no general proof catalogue hidden in this guide. A page plan must supply or point to the actual approved proof, its status, and any permission or anonymity boundary. If that evidence is not supplied, treat it as unavailable rather than reconstructing it from memory or another page.

## Review the plan

Before treating the plan as usable, ask:

- Does the page have one intelligible job and visitor action?
- Does its progression answer the visitor's practical questions without repeating the same argument?
- Are offer ownership and cross-page boundaries clear?
- Does proof appear where trust or a decision actually needs it?
- Are fixed claims and professional limits visible?
- Is adaptable material flexible enough to improve during design?
- Are open decisions genuine blockers for this page, or merely deferrable business questions?
- Could a fresh executor preserve the meaning without being handed a component specification?

Page review can revisit the same questions against final copy and renders. It should report drift to the owning authority rather than create a second plan or hidden state system.
