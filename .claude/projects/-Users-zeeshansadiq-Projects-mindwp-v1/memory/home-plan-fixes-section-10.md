`docs/HOME-PLAN.md` (header says "Don't read unless we ask") fixes the homepage's 14 sections with fixed public eyebrows/headlines. Section 10 is `CLEAR OWNERSHIP / "Know what MindWP builds, what your business owns and what happens after launch."`, and Homepage A is declared the fixed design baseline (don't redesign/merge/move/rename).

Tension the user acted on: the same plan's service-page contract lists **Ownership, handover, ongoing management as "Open" — engagement-dependent**. So a fixed section making universal ownership claims is the wrong tool. The user is reframing section 10 on the `home-b` draft — see [[home-b-section-exploration]].

**How to apply:** changing section 10 (or any fixed homepage eyebrow/heading) on the live home is a page-plan revision — surface it and get explicit sign-off; don't treat a draft experiment as an approved plan change.
